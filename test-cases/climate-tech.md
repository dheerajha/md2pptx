# Climate Technology Investment Report 2024

Tracking the flow of capital into clean energy, carbon capture, and sustainable infrastructure worldwide.

## Overview

Global climate tech investment reached **$1.8 trillion** in 2023, marking the third consecutive year of record-breaking capital deployment. Despite macroeconomic headwinds, the sector shows remarkable resilience driven by policy tailwinds from the US Inflation Reduction Act and the EU Green Deal.

## Key Drivers

Three structural forces are accelerating climate tech deployment at unprecedented scale:

- **Policy momentum** — The US IRA committed $369B to clean energy; EU Green Deal targets €1T by 2030
- **Cost deflation** — Solar LCOE fell 90% in the last decade; battery costs down 97% since 2010
- **Corporate commitments** — Over 7,000 companies have set net-zero targets covering $40T in revenue
- **Energy security** — The Russia-Ukraine conflict exposed fossil fuel dependency, boosting alternatives

## Solar & Wind: The Dominant Force

Solar and wind together captured **35% of all new power capacity** globally in 2023. For the first time, renewables added more capacity than all fossil fuel sources combined. China alone installed **217 GW** of solar in 2023, more than the entire global solar fleet in 2021.

> "We are witnessing the fastest energy transition in human history. The economics are now irreversible." — BloombergNEF, 2024 Energy Transition Report

Key milestones reached in 2023:

- Solar power became the cheapest electricity source in history — $29/MWh global average
- Offshore wind pipeline surpassed **750 GW** worldwide
- Community solar programs expanded to cover **6 million** US homes

## Electric Vehicles & Batteries

EV adoption crossed the **20% global market share** threshold in 2023, five years ahead of most forecasts. Battery energy density has doubled in the past seven years while costs plummeted.

## Green Hydrogen: Emerging Opportunity

Hydrogen investment more than doubled in 2023, driven by industrial decarbonization needs. Green hydrogen — produced via renewable-powered electrolysis — is targeting hard-to-abate sectors that electricity alone cannot reach.

Target applications include: steel manufacturing, maritime shipping, aviation fuel, and chemical feedstocks. Over **1,400 green hydrogen projects** are in various stages of development globally, with combined capacity of **270 GW** by 2030.

## Risks & Challenges

The sector faces significant near-term headwinds:

- **Supply chain constraints** — Critical mineral shortages (lithium, cobalt, rare earths) threaten scaling timelines
- **Grid integration** — Intermittency of renewables requires massive storage and grid upgrade investment
- **Permitting bottlenecks** — US renewable projects face average 5-year permitting delays
- **Financing costs** — Higher interest rates have increased the cost of capital-intensive clean energy by 15-20%
- **Political risk** — Policy reversals in key markets (US, UK) create investment uncertainty

## Outlook & Recommendations

The trajectory is clear: climate tech is transitioning from a niche sector to the dominant investment theme of the 21st century. Investors who act decisively now will capture the most asymmetric returns.

Priority investment areas for 2024-2026:

- Long-duration energy storage (target: $50-100B annual investment needed)
- Grid modernization and transmission infrastructure
- Industrial decarbonization — green steel, green cement, green chemicals
- Adaptation technologies — water security, climate-resilient agriculture
