# Global Sustainability Report 2024

Tracking corporate progress on net-zero commitments, renewable energy adoption, and ESG performance across major industries.

## The Climate Imperative

Global temperatures have risen **1.2°C** above pre-industrial levels. The IPCC warns that limiting warming to **1.5°C** requires cutting emissions by **45%** by 2030 and reaching net-zero by 2050. Every year of delay narrows the window and raises the cost of action.

> "We are the first generation to feel the impact of climate change and the last generation that can do something about it." — Former U.S. President Barack Obama

## Renewable Energy Progress

### Solar Power

Global solar capacity surpassed **1.6 terawatts** in 2023, enough to power over 400 million homes. The cost of utility-scale solar has dropped **89%** since 2010, making it the cheapest electricity source in history.

### Wind Energy

Offshore wind capacity is expected to reach **380 GW** by 2030. Leading nations include China (**310 GW** installed), USA (**145 GW**), and Germany (**68 GW**).

### Energy Storage

Battery storage costs have fallen **97%** in the last three decades. Grid-scale storage deployments grew **80%** in 2023 alone, enabling greater renewable penetration.

## Corporate ESG Performance

Companies are increasingly measured on Environmental, Social, and Governance metrics. **78%** of S&P 500 companies published sustainability reports in 2023, up from just 20% in 2011.

Top ESG performers outperform the market by an average of **4.7%** annually over a 5-year period. Institutional investors now allocate over **$35 trillion** in ESG-screened assets globally.

- **Environment** — Carbon footprint, water usage, waste reduction, biodiversity impact
- **Social** — Employee wellbeing, diversity & inclusion, community investment, supply chain ethics
- **Governance** — Board diversity, executive compensation, transparency, anti-corruption policies
- **Reporting** — GRI, SASB, TCFD, and upcoming ISSB standards alignment

## Carbon Pricing Mechanisms

### Carbon Markets

The EU Emissions Trading System (ETS) covers **40%** of EU greenhouse gas emissions. Carbon prices reached **€100/tonne** in 2023 for the first time. Voluntary carbon markets traded **$2 billion** in credits in 2023.

### Carbon Taxes

Forty-six countries now operate carbon taxes or ETS schemes covering **23%** of global emissions. Revenue from carbon pricing exceeded **$95 billion** in 2022, with many governments channeling funds into clean energy subsidies and social programs.

## Circular Economy Transition

The linear take-make-dispose model wastes **$4.5 trillion** in economic value annually. The circular economy — designing out waste and keeping materials in use — could unlock **$4.5 trillion** in new value by 2030.

Key circular strategies:
- Product-as-a-service models replacing single ownership
- Industrial symbiosis — one company's waste becomes another's input
- Regenerative agriculture rebuilding soil carbon and biodiversity
- Urban mining recovering rare earth metals from electronics

## Conclusion and Path Forward

The transition to a sustainable economy is accelerating — but not fast enough. The gap between pledges and action remains significant.

Priorities for the next decade:

- Accelerate the phase-out of coal power plants globally — deadline 2030 for OECD nations
- Scale green hydrogen production to decarbonize hard-to-abate sectors
- Mobilize **$2.4 trillion** annually in climate finance for developing nations
- Implement mandatory carbon disclosure for all publicly listed companies
- Invest in nature-based solutions to capture **10 GtCO2** per year by 2030
